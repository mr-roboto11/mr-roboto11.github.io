People to Avoid in Business

The Naysayer

via GIPHY

Just like the name entails, these are the people filled with doubt. Entreprneurs should be filled with ambition, faith, and hope. The naysayers are devoid of these three qualities and/or don't think you are able to achieve your goals. Either way, avoid this type like the black plague because they will fill your mind with doubt. They'll tell you every reason it can't be done and why you can't do it.

"They Will Fill your Mind with Doubt"

-KIN

The Hater

via GIPHY

Beware of these jealous mfs. Their feelings come from an evil place. They are mad at you and/or your entreprenuerial endeavors. They will malelovently destroy your character, reputation, business, and your dreams to shreds if you don't avoid them. They are disguised as family, friends, co-workers, and people on your social media friends list. This type is most likely to do some volatile sh*t to you; steal you items, tell blantant disrespectful lies, blame you for things you've never done, and disrespect your business any way they can. Rid your self of these cancerous bunch or you will find everything you work torwards deteriorate.

"Likely to do Some Volatile Sh*t to You"

-KIN

via GIPHY

The center of attention will need everyones admiration, likes, praises, and love. They want you to do good, just not better than them. You and your endeavors will always be a "co-star" to their ego.

Money This, Money That

Someone once told me, "Money.Is.A.Major.Issue", and you know what? He was right. I just didn't know how or what he was right about yet. This is what I gathered, it's not just all about the physical money. It's about how money makes people feel too. To a wimp, money might make he/she feel like they're better than everyone they meet. To the shopaholic, it might give them access to buy more things. I don't have to go in detail but you get the gist. PEOPLE USE MONEY TO ATTAIN THEIR DEEPEST WANTS AND DESIRES. To a real Entrepreneur or Hustler (anyone who uses their skills to get money or attain their ambitions) money is the means to make more capital.

To invest in their business or company. Business 101, right!? Don't cast your stones to swine in this situation. Having every new popular basketball shoe is not your ambition... keeping up with the Jones' is not your objective... To have a million social media friends is not your goal... having a million potential customers is! Doing what they said you couldn't do is the goal! The Entrepreneur chooses to pursue a higher calling. They didn't settle for mediocrity and decide to "take it out on the world." They chose to do what naysayers said "couldn't be done." They chose to run torwards the light, not from it. They Choose to be Fearless about their Goals!


"People who are always talking about money, they're usually never really talking about money...They're talking about what money means to them"

-KIN

Entreprenuerial Reflection

via GIPHY

Did I ask you for advice?

No, I took my own advice and researched who was knowledgeable about the topic in which I wanted to gain more knowledge about.


Did I ask for your permission?

No, I gave myself permission to work towards my goals and dreams


Did I ask you for your opinion?

No, your opinions are irrelevant and if I thought they would offer any insight to being successful, I would have ask in the beginning of my journey


Did I ask you for any money?

No, I funded myself. When I wasn't able to fund myself, I sought out the help of financial institutions and/or organizations that specialize in helping businesses in that particular sector.


Did I ask you to do my taxes?

No, I research how to properly do not only personal taxes but businesss taxes


Did I ask you to itemize my taxes?

No, I kept records of my purchases and meticulously documented the cost of my assets


Did I ask you to tutor me?

No, I self taught myself through courses, literature, and Youtube videos


Did I ask you for you expertise?

No but most people who are knowledgeable about anything will give you advice in general conversation


Did I ask for your friendship?

No, I did not become an entreprenuer to be anyone's BFF. We can network though.


Did I ask you to be a partner in my business?

No, I had a dream that I chose to WORK for, not an idea that multiple people came up with


Did I ask you to use your vehicle?

No, I caught public transportation in order to pour capital into my business. Instead of pouring all my disposable income into an asset that immediately depreciates once you purchase it.


Do I have all the answers?

No and I never pretended to


Do I owe anyone who is not an co-founder/owner, customer, employee, or investor an explanation to any of my business related matters?

No. Especially if you singlehandedly made your dreams come to fruition, put the hours of studying in, put the work in, and personally funded the endeavor!
